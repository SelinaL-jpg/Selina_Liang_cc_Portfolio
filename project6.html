Data_Portrait

This data portrait is a line chart representing my heart rate, recorded three times a day (morning, afternoon, and evening) over seven days. The line shows the highest, average, and lowest heart rates for each day. The line connects three points, each representing heart rate data for the morning, afternoon, and evening. The size of the circles shows the heart rate value—higher heart rates are represented by bigger circles. You can click to switch between different days to see how the heart rates change over time. Through this process, I learned that my heart rate changes significantly at different times of the day, especially in the morning and evening. This made me realize how different factors, like stress or activity, may affect my heart rate. Data visualization made it easy to understand the patterns in my heart rate. However, it does not show the context behind the data—such as how I felt emotionally or physically at the time of each measurement.
